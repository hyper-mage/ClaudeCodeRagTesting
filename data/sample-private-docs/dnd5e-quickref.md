A hand-written cheat-sheet for the most common rules a player or DM looks up at the table. Derived from SRD 5.1; abbreviated for table speed, not for completeness. For full rules, consult the SRD or a Player's Handbook.

---

## Ability Checks

An ability check tests a character's innate talent and training against a difficulty class (DC).

**To make an ability check:**
1. Roll 1d20.
2. Add the relevant ability modifier (STR, DEX, CON, INT, WIS, CHA).
3. Add proficiency bonus if proficient in the relevant skill or tool.
4. Compare total to the DC. Equal or higher = success.

**Typical DCs:**
| Task difficulty | DC |
|-----------------|----|
| Very easy | 5 |
| Easy | 10 |
| Medium | 15 |
| Hard | 20 |
| Very hard | 25 |
| Nearly impossible | 30 |

**Contested checks:** When two creatures oppose each other, both roll; the higher total wins. Ties = no change.

**Passive checks:** 10 + all modifiers that would normally apply. Use for repeated tasks (perception walking through a dungeon) or to determine success without rolling.

**Group checks:** Each member rolls; if at least half succeed, the whole group succeeds.

## Saving Throws

A saving throw represents an attempt to resist a spell, trap, poison, disease, or similar threat.

**To make a saving throw:**
1. Roll 1d20.
2. Add the relevant ability modifier.
3. Add proficiency bonus if proficient in that saving throw (class grants two proficiencies at level 1).
4. Compare to the DC stated by the effect. Equal or higher = success.

**Save DC for a spell or class feature:** 8 + ability modifier + proficiency bonus.

**Common save associations:**
- STR: grapples, being moved against your will
- DEX: dodging area effects (fireball, dragon breath)
- CON: poison, disease, exhaustion, concentration
- INT: psychic effects, illusions
- WIS: charms, frights, compulsions
- CHA: banishment, possession

**Concentration:** When you take damage while concentrating on a spell, make a CON save (DC 10 or half the damage taken, whichever is higher). Failure ends concentration.

## Advantage and Disadvantage

**Advantage:** Roll 2d20; take the higher.
**Disadvantage:** Roll 2d20; take the lower.

Multiple sources of advantage or disadvantage do not stack — you have it or you don't. If you have both advantage and disadvantage, they cancel out and you roll one d20 normally, regardless of how many of each source apply.

**Common sources of advantage:**
- Helping another creature with a task (one helper, action required)
- Hidden attacker
- Prone target attacked from melee
- Restrained target
- Spells or features that explicitly grant it (Bless, Guidance, Faerie Fire)

**Common sources of disadvantage:**
- Attacking at long range with a ranged weapon
- Attacking a creature you can't see
- Prone attacker attacking at range or beyond 5 ft melee
- Heavily obscured target
- Frightened or poisoned condition on the attacker

## Combat Actions

On your turn you take one action, one bonus action (if you have one available), can move up to your speed, and can use one reaction per round.

### Attack
Make one melee or ranged weapon attack. The Extra Attack feature lets some classes attack more than once per Attack action.

- **Melee attack roll:** 1d20 + STR mod (or DEX for finesse) + proficiency.
- **Ranged attack roll:** 1d20 + DEX mod + proficiency.
- **Damage on hit:** weapon die + same ability mod used for the attack.
- **Critical hit:** natural 20 on the d20 — roll all weapon damage dice twice, then add modifiers once.
- **Critical miss:** natural 1 always misses; no extra penalty by default.

### Cast a Spell
Cast a spell with a casting time of 1 action. Most spells require components: V (verbal), S (somatic), M (material). You can cast one leveled spell per turn — bonus action spells and action spells cannot both be leveled in the same turn (the "one spell per turn" rule), except cantrips which are always allowed.

### Dash
Gain extra movement equal to your speed for the current turn (after modifiers from being prone, difficult terrain, etc.).

### Disengage
Your movement does not provoke opportunity attacks for the rest of the turn.

### Dodge
Until the start of your next turn, attack rolls against you have disadvantage and you make DEX saves with advantage. You lose this benefit if you become incapacitated or your speed drops to 0.

### Help
Either (a) the target gets advantage on the next ability check they make to perform a task you are helping with, or (b) the next attack roll against a creature within 5 ft of you (made by an ally before the start of your next turn) has advantage.

### Hide
Make a DEX (Stealth) check. If you beat the passive Perception of every creature that could see you, you become hidden. You can't hide from a creature that can see you clearly.

### Ready
Choose a trigger ("when the orc opens the door") and an action or move (no longer than a single action; "I cast Magic Missile"). When the trigger fires before your next turn, your reaction triggers the readied action. Concentration applies if a spell is involved.

### Search
Devote your attention to finding something — WIS (Perception) for spotting, INT (Investigation) for deducing.

### Use an Object
Interact with a second object on your turn (the first interaction is free as part of your move or action). Examples: drawing a second weapon, opening a second door, drinking a potion.

### Bonus actions
Only granted by class features, spells, or magic items. Examples: Cunning Action (Rogue), Two-Weapon Fighting, Healing Word (Cleric).

### Reactions
One per round. Triggered by a specific event. Most common: opportunity attack (when a hostile creature leaves your melee reach without disengaging).

### Movement details
- **Difficult terrain:** each foot costs 2 ft of movement.
- **Climbing, swimming, crawling:** each foot costs 2 ft of movement (1 ft extra).
- **Standing from prone:** costs half your speed.
- **Long jump:** STR score in feet with a 10 ft running start (half if standing).
- **High jump:** 3 + STR mod in feet with a 10 ft running start (half if standing).

## Conditions

A condition alters a creature's capabilities until the cause ends or a remedy is applied. Unless specified, conditions are not cumulative.

### Blinded
- Cannot see; auto-fails ability checks that require sight.
- Attack rolls against the creature have advantage.
- The creature's attack rolls have disadvantage.

### Charmed
- Cannot attack the charmer or target them with harmful abilities/effects.
- The charmer has advantage on social ability checks against the creature.

### Deafened
- Cannot hear; auto-fails ability checks that require hearing.

### Frightened
- Disadvantage on ability checks and attack rolls while the source of fear is in line of sight.
- Cannot willingly move closer to the source.

### Grappled
- Speed becomes 0 and cannot benefit from any bonus to speed.
- Condition ends if the grappler is incapacitated or if removed from reach.

### Incapacitated
- Cannot take actions or reactions.

### Invisible
- Considered heavily obscured for purposes of being seen. Location can still be detected by noise or tracks.
- Attack rolls against the creature have disadvantage.
- The creature's attack rolls have advantage.

### Paralyzed
- Incapacitated (cannot take actions or reactions) and cannot move or speak.
- Auto-fails STR and DEX saving throws.
- Attack rolls against the creature have advantage.
- Any attack that hits the creature within 5 ft is a critical hit.

### Petrified
- Transformed, with all carried gear, into solid inanimate substance (usually stone). Weight increases tenfold. Stops aging.
- Incapacitated, cannot move or speak, unaware of surroundings.
- Attack rolls against the creature have advantage.
- Auto-fails STR and DEX saving throws.
- Resistance to all damage.
- Immune to poison and disease, though existing ones are suspended.

### Poisoned
- Disadvantage on attack rolls and ability checks.

### Prone
- Only movement option is to crawl unless the creature stands up (costs half its speed).
- Disadvantage on attack rolls.
- Attack rolls against the creature have advantage if the attacker is within 5 ft; otherwise disadvantage.

### Restrained
- Speed becomes 0 and cannot benefit from bonuses to speed.
- Attack rolls against the creature have advantage; its attack rolls have disadvantage.
- Disadvantage on DEX saving throws.

### Stunned
- Incapacitated, cannot move, can speak only falteringly.
- Auto-fails STR and DEX saving throws.
- Attack rolls against the creature have advantage.

### Unconscious
- Incapacitated, cannot move or speak, unaware of surroundings.
- Drops what it is holding and falls prone.
- Auto-fails STR and DEX saving throws.
- Attack rolls against the creature have advantage.
- Any attack that hits within 5 ft is a critical hit.

### Exhaustion
Six levels of severity. Each level applies cumulatively with the previous.
1. Disadvantage on ability checks.
2. Speed halved.
3. Disadvantage on attack rolls and saving throws.
4. HP maximum halved.
5. Speed reduced to 0.
6. Death.

A long rest removes one level; Greater Restoration removes one level. Some classes (Barbarian) accrue exhaustion from features.

## Rests

### Short rest
- At least 1 hour of light activity. No fighting.
- Spend Hit Dice to recover HP (each die rolled adds CON mod; minimum 1 per die).
- Some class features recharge (Warlock spell slots, Fighter Action Surge in some subclasses, ki points for Monks at higher levels — check class).

### Long rest
- At least 8 hours: 6 hours sleep + up to 2 hours light activity. One per 24-hour period.
- Recover all HP.
- Recover half of total Hit Dice (minimum 1).
- Reset most class resources (all spell slots, Channel Divinity, Wild Shape, etc.).
- A rest interrupted by ≥1 hour of combat, casting, or strenuous activity does not count.

## Death and Dying

- **0 HP:** drop to 0 HP, fall unconscious. If damage equals or exceeds your HP maximum at any time, you die instantly.
- **Death saving throws:** at the start of each turn while at 0 HP, roll 1d20. 10+ = success, <10 = failure. 3 successes = stable. 3 failures = dead.
- **Natural 20 on death save:** regain 1 HP.
- **Natural 1 on death save:** counts as 2 failures.
- **Damage at 0 HP:** counts as 1 failure (or 2 if a critical hit).
- **Stabilizing:** an ally can use an action to make a DC 10 WIS (Medicine) check to stabilize. A stable creature still needs 1d4 hours before regaining 1 HP and consciousness.

## Spellcasting Quick Notes

- **Cantrips:** spells of level 0 — always prepared, unlimited use.
- **Spell slots:** consumed when casting. Higher-level slots can upcast lower-level spells.
- **Concentration:** at most one concentration spell active at a time. Casting a new concentration spell ends the previous one.
- **Ritual casting:** spells with the ritual tag can be cast without expending a slot if the caster has the ritual-casting class feature; adds 10 minutes to casting time.
- **Counterspell:** reaction (3rd-level slot). Auto-counters spells of level 3 or lower. For higher-level spells, roll an ability check: DC 10 + spell's level, using your spellcasting ability.

## Initiative and Surprise

- **Initiative:** at the start of combat, every creature rolls 1d20 + DEX mod. Highest goes first. Ties handled by DM (or by DEX score).
- **Surprise:** if a side fails to notice opponents at the start of combat, surprised creatures cannot take an action or reaction on their first turn. Make a Stealth check against the passive Perception of each opponent to determine surprise.

---

## Cover

- **Half cover:** +2 to AC and DEX saves.
- **Three-quarters cover:** +5 to AC and DEX saves.
- **Total cover:** cannot be targeted directly by an attack or a spell.

Cover is determined from the attacker's perspective relative to the target.

---

## Resistance, Vulnerability, Immunity

- **Resistance:** take half damage (rounded down) of the resisted type.
- **Vulnerability:** take double damage of the type.
- **Immunity:** take no damage from the type. Immune to a condition = the condition has no effect.
- Multiple resistances or vulnerabilities to the same type do not stack — resistance and vulnerability cancel each other, leaving normal damage.

---

## Inspiration

A DM can award Inspiration for good roleplay. A character with Inspiration can spend it to gain advantage on one ability check, attack roll, or saving throw. Either you have it or you don't — no stacking.
