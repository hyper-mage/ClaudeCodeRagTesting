# 7 Wonders

## Overview

7 Wonders, designed by Antoine Bauza and published in 2010, is a card drafting and civilization building game for 2-7 players. Each player leads an ancient civilization, developing their city and constructing one of the seven wonders of the ancient world across three ages of play. A typical game lasts 30-45 minutes regardless of player count, thanks to the simultaneous play mechanism. The game won the Kennerspiel des Jahres award in 2011 and is widely regarded as one of the best card drafting games ever made.

The simultaneous card selection keeps all players engaged at all times, eliminating downtime even at higher player counts. Strategic depth comes from tracking what cards your neighbors are drafting, managing resource production chains, and timing your wonder construction.

## Setup

1. Randomly deal one wonder board to each player. Each player places their board face up in front of them with the chosen side (A for beginners, B for experienced players) visible. Each wonder board represents a different ancient civilization with unique wonder stages.
2. Sort the age cards by their backs (Age I, Age II, Age III). For each age, remove cards based on player count: use only the cards marked for the appropriate number of players. Shuffle each age deck separately.
3. Place the conflict tokens nearby, sorted by value: Age I tokens show +1/-1, Age II tokens show +3/-1, and Age III tokens show +5/-1.
4. Give each player 3 coins from the bank as starting wealth.
5. Deal 7 Age I cards to each player as their starting hand.
6. Each player places their wonder board between themselves and their neighbors, establishing the left and right neighbor relationships that persist throughout the game.

## Turn Structure

7 Wonders is played over three ages, each consisting of 6 turns. All players take their turns simultaneously:

1. **Choose a card.** Each player selects one card from their hand to play, placing it face down in front of them. All players reveal their chosen cards at the same time.

2. **Play the card.** Each player resolves their chosen card in one of three ways. First, they may build the structure by paying its resource cost (using resources from their own city, purchasing resources from neighbors for 2 coins each, or using commerce discounts). Built structures provide resources, military strength, science symbols, civic victory points, or commercial benefits. Second, they may use the card to build a stage of their wonder by tucking the card face down under the next uncompleted stage of their wonder board and paying the wonder stage's resource cost, gaining its depicted benefit. Third, they may discard the card to receive 3 coins from the bank.

3. **Pass remaining cards.** After all players have resolved their cards, each player passes their remaining hand to their neighbor. In Ages I and III, hands pass to the left (clockwise). In Age II, hands pass to the right (counterclockwise). This drafting mechanism means players must consider which cards they are passing to opponents.

4. **Resolve military conflicts (end of each age only).** After the 6th turn of each age, each player compares their military strength (shield symbols) with each of their two neighbors. For each comparison, the player with more shields takes a victory token for that age. The player with fewer shields takes a defeat token worth -1 point. Ties result in no tokens for either player.

## Win Conditions

After the third age and final military conflicts are resolved, players tally their victory points from seven categories: military conflict tokens (net of victories and defeats), treasury (1 point per 3 coins), wonder stages (points shown on built stages), civilian structures (blue cards with fixed victory points), scientific structures (green cards scoring by sets and collections: each set of 3 different symbols scores 7 points, plus each group of identical symbols scores points equal to the quantity squared), commercial structures (some yellow cards grant end-game points), and guilds (purple cards from Age III that score based on specific conditions involving neighbors). The player with the most total victory points wins. Ties are broken by the player with the most coins remaining.

## Components

- 7 wonder boards (double-sided with A and B sides)
- 148 age cards (49 Age I, 49 Age II, 50 Age III including guilds)
- 46 conflict tokens (victories and defeats for three ages)
- 24 value-1 coins and 20 value-3 coins
- 1 scorepad
- 1 rules booklet
- 2 player aid cards
