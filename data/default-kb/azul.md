# Azul

## Overview

Azul, designed by Michael Kiesling and published in 2017, is an abstract tile-drafting and pattern-building game for 2-4 players. Inspired by the Portuguese azulejo tiles that adorn the walls of the Royal Palace of Evora, players take turns drafting colored tiles from factory displays and placing them on their personal player boards to score points. A typical game lasts 30-45 minutes. Azul won the Spiel des Jahres award in 2018 and is praised for its elegant combination of tactical drafting and spatial planning.

The game offers satisfying tactile gameplay with its chunky resin tiles and creates interesting tension between taking tiles you want and denying tiles to opponents. Simple rules combined with deep strategic choices make it accessible to new players while rewarding experienced ones.

## Setup

1. Give each player a personal player board. The board shows a wall pattern on the right side (a 5x5 grid where each row contains each of the 5 colors exactly once in a fixed pattern) and five pattern lines on the left side (rows of increasing length from 1 to 5 spaces).
2. Place the round scoring marker on each player's board at the 0 position on the scoring track.
3. Place the factory displays in the center of the table. Use 5 displays for 2 players, 7 for 3 players, or 9 for 4 players. Each factory display is a circular cardboard disc.
4. Place all 100 tiles (20 each in 5 colors: blue, yellow, red, black, and white) in the cloth bag and mix them thoroughly.
5. Draw 4 tiles randomly from the bag and place them on each factory display.
6. Place the first player marker (a large "1" tile) in the center of the table area between the factory displays. This area is called the center of the table.
7. The last player to have visited Portugal goes first, or determine the starting player randomly.

## Turn Structure

The game is played over multiple rounds, each consisting of two phases:

**Factory Offer Phase:** Players take turns in clockwise order, each performing one action:

1. **Pick tiles from a factory display.** Choose one factory display and take all tiles of one color from it. Move any remaining tiles on that display to the center of the table. Alternatively, take all tiles of one color from the center of the table. The first player to take tiles from the center also takes the first player marker and places it in their floor line (penalty row), which will cost them 1 point but grants first player position next round.

2. **Place tiles on a pattern line.** Place the drafted tiles from left to right on one of the five pattern lines on the left side of your player board. All tiles placed in a turn must go on the same row. A pattern line can only contain tiles of a single color, and the color must match an empty space in the corresponding wall row. If you draft more tiles than the pattern line can hold, excess tiles go to your floor line as penalties. If no pattern line can legally accept the tiles, all tiles go to the floor line.

The Factory Offer Phase continues until all tiles have been drafted from all factory displays and the center of the table.

**Wall Tiling Phase:** All players simultaneously resolve their boards:

1. For each completed pattern line (all spaces filled), move one tile from that line to the matching color space in the corresponding wall row. Score 1 point if the tile is isolated, or score the total count of connected tiles in the horizontal and vertical line through the placed tile. Clear the remaining tiles from that pattern line to the game box lid (discard).
2. Incomplete pattern lines remain as they are for the next round.
3. Lose points for tiles in the floor line: -1 for the first two tiles, -2 each for the next two, -3 each for any beyond that. Clear the floor line.
4. Refill factory displays from the bag for the next round.

## Win Conditions

The game ends at the conclusion of the round in which any player completes at least one horizontal row of 5 tiles on their wall. Final bonus scoring is then applied: 2 points for each complete horizontal row of 5 tiles, 7 points for each complete vertical column of 5 tiles, and 10 points for each color where all 5 tiles of that color have been placed on the wall. The player with the highest total score wins. In case of a tie, the player with the most complete horizontal rows wins.

## Components

- 100 resin tiles (20 each in 5 colors: blue, yellow, red, black, white)
- 4 player boards (double-sided: standard and variant)
- 5 to 9 factory display discs (number varies by player count)
- 1 cloth bag for tile drawing
- 1 first player marker
- 4 scoring markers
- 1 rules booklet
