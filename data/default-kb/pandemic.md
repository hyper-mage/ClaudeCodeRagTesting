# Pandemic

## Overview

Pandemic, designed by Matt Leacock and published in 2008, is a cooperative board game for 2-4 players. Players work together as members of a disease control team, traveling the world to treat infections and discover cures for four deadly diseases before they spread out of control. A typical game lasts 45-60 minutes. Unlike most board games, all players either win together or lose together, making communication and strategic coordination essential.

The game is widely praised for pioneering the cooperative genre in modern board gaming. Its escalating tension as outbreaks chain across the globe creates memorable moments, and the different role abilities encourage teamwork and discussion.

## Setup

1. Place the board in the center of the table, showing a world map with 48 cities connected by routes.
2. Place the 4 disease cure markers on the board's cure indicator area, uncured side up.
3. Set the outbreak marker on the 0 space of the outbreak track.
4. Set the infection rate marker on the first position of the infection rate track (showing 2).
5. Place research stations: put one research station in Atlanta (the CDC headquarters).
6. Separate the infection cards and shuffle them. Draw 3 cards and place 3 disease cubes on each city drawn. Draw 3 more cards and place 2 cubes on each. Draw 3 more cards and place 1 cube on each. Place all drawn infection cards in the infection discard pile.
7. Shuffle the role cards and deal one to each player. Each player takes the matching colored pawn and places it in Atlanta.
8. Prepare the player deck: deal the starting hands (4 cards each for 2 players, 3 cards each for 3 players, 2 cards each for 4 players). Divide the remaining player cards into equal piles based on difficulty (4 piles for introductory, 5 for standard, 6 for heroic), shuffle one epidemic card into each pile, then stack the piles to form the player draw deck.

## Turn Structure

Each player's turn consists of three phases performed in order:

1. **Take 4 actions.** The active player may perform any combination of 4 actions, repeating actions if desired. Available actions include: drive/ferry to an adjacent connected city, take a direct flight by discarding the card of the destination city, take a charter flight by discarding the card of the city you are currently in to fly anywhere, take a shuttle flight between two cities that both have research stations, build a research station by discarding the card matching your current city, treat disease by removing one disease cube from your current city (or all cubes of a cured disease), share knowledge by giving or taking the card matching your current city to or from another player in the same city, and discover a cure by discarding 5 cards of the same color at a research station.

2. **Draw 2 player cards.** Draw the top 2 cards from the player deck. If an epidemic card is drawn, resolve it immediately: advance the infection rate marker, draw the bottom card of the infection deck and place 3 disease cubes on that city (causing an outbreak if it already has 3), then shuffle the infection discard pile and place it on top of the infection deck. If a player ever has more than 7 cards after drawing, they must immediately discard or play cards down to 7.

3. **Infect cities.** Draw cards from the top of the infection deck equal to the current infection rate (initially 2) and place 1 disease cube of the matching color on each city drawn. If a city already has 3 cubes of that color, an outbreak occurs instead: the outbreak marker advances and 1 cube of that color is placed in every city connected to the outbreak city, potentially triggering chain outbreaks.

## Win Conditions

Players win by discovering cures for all 4 diseases. A cure is discovered when a player discards 5 cards of the same color at a research station. Diseases do not need to be eradicated (all cubes removed) to win, only cured. Players lose if any of three conditions occur: the outbreak marker reaches 8, there are not enough disease cubes to place when needed (any single color runs out), or there are not enough player cards to draw. There is no way to partially win: the team succeeds together or fails together.

## Components

- 1 game board with world map
- 7 role cards with matching pawns
- 59 player cards (48 city cards, 6 epidemic cards, 5 event cards)
- 48 infection cards
- 96 disease cubes (24 in each of 4 colors)
- 4 cure markers
- 1 outbreak marker
- 1 infection rate marker
- 6 research stations
