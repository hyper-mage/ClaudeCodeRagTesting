# Splendor

## Overview

Splendor, designed by Marc Andre and published in 2014, is an engine-building card game for 2-4 players. Players take on the role of Renaissance merchants, collecting gem tokens and purchasing development cards to build a gem-producing economic engine. As their purchasing power grows, players attract the attention of wealthy nobles who visit to bestow prestige points. A typical game lasts 30 minutes. Splendor was nominated for the Spiel des Jahres award in 2014 and is known for its satisfying economic gameplay and high-quality weighted poker chip tokens.

The game creates a compelling progression as players start with nothing and gradually build up gem discounts that make expensive cards affordable. The race to reach 15 prestige points creates tension, especially as multiple players compete for the same noble tiles and high-value cards.

## Setup

1. Shuffle each of the three development card decks separately (Level 1, Level 2, Level 3, identifiable by the number of dots on the card back). Place each deck face down in a column in the center of the table with Level 1 at the bottom, Level 2 in the middle, and Level 3 at the top.
2. Reveal 4 cards from each deck and place them in a row next to their respective deck, forming a 3x4 grid of available development cards.
3. Shuffle the noble tiles and reveal a number equal to the number of players plus 1. For example, reveal 4 noble tiles in a 3-player game. Return unused noble tiles to the box without looking at them.
4. Place the gem tokens in the center of the table in 6 stacks by type. For 2 players, use 4 tokens of each gem color; for 3 players, use 5; for 4 players, use 7 of each. Always use all 5 gold tokens regardless of player count. The five gem colors are diamond (white), sapphire (blue), emerald (green), ruby (red), and onyx (black).
5. The youngest player goes first, with play proceeding clockwise.

## Turn Structure

On each turn, a player must perform exactly one of four actions:

1. **Take 3 gem tokens of different colors.** Choose 3 different gem token stacks and take 1 token from each. You cannot take gold tokens with this action. You may take fewer than 3 if fewer than 3 colors are available.

2. **Take 2 gem tokens of the same color.** Take 2 tokens from a single gem stack, but only if that stack has at least 4 tokens before taking. You cannot take gold tokens with this action.

3. **Reserve a development card.** Take 1 face-up development card or the top card of any deck and add it to your hand (maximum 3 reserved cards). You also receive 1 gold token from the supply, which acts as a wild gem for purchases. If no gold tokens remain, you still reserve the card but receive no gold.

4. **Purchase a development card.** Buy 1 face-up development card or 1 card from your reserved hand by paying its gem cost. Each development card you already own provides a permanent discount of 1 gem of its bonus color. Gold tokens may substitute for any gem. Spent tokens return to the supply. The purchased card is placed in front of you, providing its permanent gem bonus and any prestige points printed on it.

After performing an action, if a player has more than 10 gem tokens total, they must return tokens to the supply until they have exactly 10. Then check for noble visits: if a player meets the requirements shown on any noble tile (specific combinations of development card bonuses), that noble tile is taken and placed in front of the player, granting its prestige points. If multiple nobles qualify, the player chooses one. Replace any purchased face-up development card with a new one from the corresponding deck.

## Win Conditions

The game end is triggered when any player reaches 15 or more prestige points at the end of their turn. Complete the current round so that all players have had an equal number of turns. The player with the most prestige points wins. Prestige points come from development cards and noble tiles. In case of a tie, the player who purchased fewer development cards wins, as they achieved the same score with greater efficiency.

## Components

- 90 development cards (40 Level 1, 30 Level 2, 20 Level 3)
- 10 noble tiles
- 40 gem tokens (7 each of 5 colors for 4-player game, plus extras for smaller games)
- 5 gold joker tokens
- 1 rules booklet
