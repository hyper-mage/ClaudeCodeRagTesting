# Codenames

## Overview

Codenames, designed by Vlaada Chvatil and published in 2015, is a word association party game for 2-8 players, best played with 4 or more. Two teams compete to identify their agents from a grid of 25 words using one-word clues given by their team's spymaster. A typical game lasts 15-30 minutes. The game won the Spiel des Jahres award in 2016 and has become one of the most popular party games worldwide.

Codenames excels at creating moments of tension, laughter, and clever wordplay. The spymaster role demands creative thinking to connect multiple words with a single clue, while field operatives must interpret clues carefully to avoid picking the other team's agents or the deadly assassin.

## Setup

1. Arrange 25 word cards in a 5x5 grid in the center of the table, with each card showing one word visible to all players.
2. Divide players into two teams, red and blue. Each team chooses one player to be the spymaster. The remaining players on each team are field operatives.
3. The two spymasters sit on the same side of the table, facing their teammates across the grid.
4. Draw one key card at random and place it in the stand between the two spymasters so only they can see it. The key card shows a 5x5 pattern indicating which words belong to the red team (8 or 9 words), the blue team (8 or 9 words), which are innocent bystanders (7 words), and which one is the assassin.
5. The key card indicates which team goes first by highlighting that team's color on the border. The starting team has 9 agents to find while the other team has 8.
6. Place the agent cards (red, blue, bystander, and assassin) in a pile accessible to the spymasters for covering guessed words.

## Turn Structure

Teams alternate turns, beginning with the team indicated by the key card:

1. **Spymaster gives a clue.** The spymaster says a single word and a number. The word is a clue that relates to one or more of their team's words on the grid. The number tells the team how many words on the grid relate to that clue. The clue word must not be any word visible on the grid and must not share a root with any grid word. The spymaster must keep a straight face and not give any additional hints through body language or tone.

2. **Field operatives guess.** The team discusses and then touches one word card on the grid as their guess. The spymaster covers the guessed word with the appropriate agent card: red agent, blue agent, innocent bystander, or assassin. If the guess matches one of their team's words, the team may continue guessing. They may make up to one more guess than the number given with the clue. If they guess an innocent bystander or the other team's word, their turn ends immediately. If they guess the assassin, their team immediately loses the game.

3. **Optional stop.** The team may voluntarily stop guessing at any time after making at least one guess, even if they have guesses remaining. Saved guesses do not carry over but the extra guess from the clue number allows teams to catch up on previously missed words.

## Win Conditions

A team wins by identifying all of their agents on the grid. The game ends immediately when the last agent of either color is revealed, and that team wins regardless of whose turn it is. A team also loses immediately if one of their field operatives touches the assassin card, awarding victory to the opposing team. If all agents of one color are covered through the other team accidentally guessing them, that team still wins. The game cannot end in a draw.

## Components

- 200 double-sided word cards (400 total words)
- 40 key cards showing agent positions
- 8 red agent cards
- 8 blue agent cards
- 7 innocent bystander cards
- 1 double agent card (for the starting team's extra agent)
- 1 assassin card
- 1 card stand for the key card
- 1 timer (optional, for competitive play)
- 1 rules booklet
