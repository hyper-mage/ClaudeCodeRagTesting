# Wingspan

## Overview

Wingspan, designed by Elizabeth Hargrave and published in 2019, is an engine-building card game for 1-5 players themed around birdwatching and ornithology. Players attract birds to their wildlife preserves across three different habitat types, building increasingly powerful combinations as birds chain together. A typical game lasts 40-70 minutes. Wingspan won the Kennerspiel des Jahres award in 2019 and is praised for its artwork, bird facts, and innovative gameplay.

The game stands out for its unique theme and production quality, featuring 170 unique bird cards with accurate illustrations and real species information.

## Setup

1. Place the bird tray in the center of the table. Shuffle the bird card deck and fill the bird tray with 3 face-up bird cards.
2. Place the bird feeder dice tower on the table. Roll all 5 food dice and place them in the bird feeder.
3. Shuffle the bonus cards and deal 2 to each player. Each player will keep 1 bonus card and discard the other. Bonus cards provide end-of-game scoring objectives for specific types of birds.
4. Deal 5 bird cards to each player. Players will keep a combination of bird cards and food tokens totaling 5 (for example, keep 3 birds and 2 food tokens, or 2 birds and 3 food tokens). Return unchosen birds to the deck and unchosen food to the supply.
5. Give each player a player mat showing three habitat rows: forest (food gathering), grassland (egg laying), and wetland (card drawing). Each row has 5 spaces for birds.
6. Place the egg supply, food token supply, and goal board within reach of all players.
7. Place the round goal tiles: randomly select 1 for each of the 4 rounds and place them on the goal board. These provide competitive scoring goals each round.
8. Give each player 8 action cubes of their chosen color. In round 1, players have 8 turns; this decreases by 1 each round as cubes are placed on the goal board.
9. The player who most recently went birdwatching goes first.

## Turn Structure

On each turn, a player places one action cube on their player mat and performs one of four actions:

1. **Play a bird.** Choose a bird card from your hand and pay its food cost using food tokens from your supply. Additionally, if the column in the chosen habitat row already contains birds, pay 1 egg for the second column, 2 eggs for the third column, and so on. Place the bird card in the leftmost empty space in the chosen habitat row. Some birds have "when played" abilities that activate immediately.

2. **Gain food.** Place your action cube on the leftmost exposed space in the forest row. Take 1 food die from the bird feeder matching the food type you want. Then activate any bird abilities in the forest row from right to left. Each bird with an "when activated" ability triggers in sequence, potentially providing bonus food, cards, or other effects. If the bird feeder is empty or contains only one food type, reroll all dice before taking.

3. **Lay eggs.** Place your action cube on the leftmost exposed space in the grassland row. Take the indicated number of eggs from the supply and place them on any birds you control that have egg capacity remaining. Then activate bird abilities in the grassland row from right to left. Eggs serve as both a cost to play birds in later columns and as victory points.

4. **Draw bird cards.** Place your action cube on the leftmost exposed space in the wetland row. Draw the indicated number of bird cards from the face-up tray or the top of the deck. Then activate bird abilities in the wetland row from right to left.

As you play more birds in a habitat, the base action for that habitat becomes weaker (later spaces give fewer resources) but you activate more bird abilities, creating an engine-building progression.

## Win Conditions

The game ends after 4 rounds. At the end of each round, players compete for the round goal (such as most birds in a specific habitat or most eggs in a row) and place one action cube on the goal board, reducing their available turns for subsequent rounds. Final scoring tallies points from five sources: points printed on each bird card played, eggs on birds (1 point per egg), food tokens cached on bird abilities (1 point per cached food), tucked cards under bird abilities (1 point per tucked card), and bonus card objectives completed. Round goal points are also added based on ranking in each round's objective. The player with the most total points wins. Ties are broken by the number of unused food tokens.

## Components

- 170 unique bird cards with artwork and real species information
- 26 bonus cards
- 16 automa cards (for solo play)
- 75 miniature eggs in 5 colors
- 5 custom food dice
- 1 bird feeder dice tower
- 5 player mats
- 40 action cubes (8 per player)
- 1 goal board with 4 round goal tiles (8 tiles total, double-sided)
- 1 scorepad
- 1 bird tray for card display
- 103 food tokens (5 types)
- 1 first player token
- 1 rules booklet
