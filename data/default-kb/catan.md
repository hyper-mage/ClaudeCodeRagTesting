# Catan

## Overview

Catan, designed by Klaus Teuber and first published in 1995, is a resource management and trading game for 3-4 players. Players take on the role of settlers establishing colonies on the island of Catan, competing to build the most successful settlement network. A typical game lasts 60-90 minutes. The game is widely regarded as one of the most influential modern board games and has sold over 30 million copies worldwide.

Catan combines strategic planning with negotiation and a touch of luck. The modular hex-tile board ensures every game has a different layout, keeping the experience fresh even after many plays. Players must balance expanding their settlements with managing limited resources, all while negotiating trades with opponents.

## Setup

1. Assemble the frame border pieces to create the ocean boundary.
2. Randomly place the 19 terrain hex tiles inside the frame: 4 forest, 4 pasture, 4 fields, 3 hills, 3 mountains, and 1 desert.
3. Place number tokens (2-12, excluding 7) on each terrain hex in alphabetical order, skipping the desert.
4. Each player chooses a color and takes 5 settlements, 4 cities, and 15 roads of that color.
5. Place the robber pawn on the desert hex.
6. Shuffle the development card deck and place it face down nearby.
7. Sort resource cards (brick, lumber, wool, grain, ore) into five supply stacks.
8. Each player places two settlements and two roads on the board during the initial placement phase, following intersection placement rules. No two settlements may be adjacent.
9. The player who placed last collects one resource card for each terrain hex adjacent to their second settlement.

## Turn Structure

1. **Roll the dice.** The active player rolls two six-sided dice. The sum determines which terrain hexes produce resources. Every player with a settlement adjacent to a hex matching the number rolled receives one resource card of that type. Cities produce two resource cards instead of one.
2. **Robber activation.** If a 7 is rolled, the robber activates. Any player holding more than 7 resource cards must discard half (rounded down). The active player then moves the robber to any terrain hex and steals one random resource card from a player with a settlement or city adjacent to that hex.
3. **Trade.** The active player may trade resource cards with other players at any mutually agreed rate. Players may also trade with the bank at a default 4:1 ratio, or at better rates (3:1 or 2:1) if they have settlements on harbor locations.
4. **Build.** The active player may spend resource cards to build roads (1 brick + 1 lumber), settlements (1 brick + 1 lumber + 1 wool + 1 grain), cities (2 grain + 3 ore), or purchase development cards (1 wool + 1 grain + 1 ore). Settlements must be placed at intersections connected by the player's roads and at least two edges away from any other settlement.
5. **Play development cards.** The active player may play one development card per turn (knight, road building, year of plenty, monopoly, or victory point). Knight cards move the robber. Development cards cannot be played on the turn they were purchased.

## Win Conditions

The first player to reach 10 victory points on their turn wins the game. Victory points are earned through building settlements (1 point each), upgrading to cities (2 points each), holding the Longest Road card (2 points, requires at least 5 consecutive road segments), holding the Largest Army card (2 points, requires at least 3 knight cards played), and collecting victory point development cards (1 point each). Victory point cards are kept hidden until a player can claim victory.

## Components

- 19 terrain hex tiles (forest, pasture, fields, hills, mountains, desert)
- 6 sea frame border pieces
- 18 number tokens
- 95 resource cards (brick, lumber, wool, grain, ore)
- 25 development cards (14 knights, 6 progress, 5 victory points)
- 4 sets of player pieces (each with 5 settlements, 4 cities, 15 roads)
- 2 special cards (Longest Road, Largest Army)
- 1 robber pawn
- 2 six-sided dice
- 1 building costs reference card per player
