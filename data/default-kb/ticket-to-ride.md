# Ticket to Ride

## Overview

Ticket to Ride, designed by Alan R. Moon and published in 2004, is a railway-themed board game for 2-5 players. Players collect train cards and claim railway routes across a map of North America, connecting distant cities to fulfill destination tickets. A typical game lasts 30-60 minutes. The game won the prestigious Spiel des Jahres award in 2004 and has become one of the best-selling modern board games.

The game is known for its simple rules and accessible gameplay, making it an excellent gateway game for new players while still offering strategic depth. The tension between claiming routes early and waiting for the right cards creates engaging decisions throughout the game.

## Setup

1. Unfold the board and place it in the center of the table, showing the map of North America with cities and routes.
2. Each player selects a color and takes 45 train car pieces of that color.
3. Shuffle the train car cards and deal 4 cards face up next to the draw pile. Place the remaining deck face down.
4. Shuffle the destination ticket cards separately.
5. Deal 3 destination ticket cards to each player. Each player must keep at least 2 of the 3 tickets and may keep all 3. Return any discarded tickets to the bottom of the destination ticket deck.
6. Place the scoring markers for each player on the start position of the scoring track along the board edge.
7. The player who has traveled the most in real life goes first, or determine the starting player randomly.

## Turn Structure

On each turn, a player must perform exactly one of three actions:

1. **Draw train car cards.** The player draws 2 train car cards. Cards may be drawn from the 5 face-up cards or from the top of the face-down deck, in any combination. If a player takes a face-up locomotive (wild) card, that counts as their entire draw for the turn and they cannot draw a second card. When a face-up card is taken, replace it immediately from the deck. If 3 or more of the 5 face-up cards are locomotives, discard all 5 face-up cards and draw 5 new ones.

2. **Claim a route.** The player plays a set of train car cards matching the color and number of spaces on a route to claim it. Place train car pieces on each space of the route. Score points immediately based on the route length: 1 space = 1 point, 2 spaces = 2 points, 3 spaces = 4 points, 4 spaces = 7 points, 5 spaces = 10 points, 6 spaces = 15 points. Locomotive cards are wild and can substitute for any color. Gray routes can be claimed with any single color. In games with 2-3 players, only one of the double routes between two cities may be claimed.

3. **Draw destination tickets.** The player draws 3 destination ticket cards from the deck and must keep at least 1. Each destination ticket shows two cities and a point value. If the player connects those cities with a continuous path of their routes by the end of the game, they score that many bonus points. If they fail to connect them, they lose that many points.

## Win Conditions

The game ends when any player has 2 or fewer train car pieces remaining at the end of their turn. Each player then gets one final turn, including the player who triggered the end. Points are tallied from three sources: route points already scored during the game, bonus points for completed destination tickets, and penalty points subtracted for incomplete destination tickets. The player who has the longest continuous path of routes receives a 10-point bonus card. The player with the highest total score wins. In case of a tie, the player who completed the most destination tickets wins.

## Components

- 1 board map of North America
- 240 colored train car pieces (45 per player in 5 colors, plus extras)
- 110 train car cards (12 of each color in 8 colors, plus 14 locomotive wilds)
- 30 destination ticket cards
- 1 longest route bonus card
- 5 wooden scoring markers
- 1 rules summary card
