# Dominion

## Overview

Dominion, designed by Donald X. Vaccarino and published in 2008, is a deck-building card game for 2-4 players. Players start with a small, identical deck of basic cards and gradually improve it by purchasing more powerful cards from a shared supply. The goal is to accumulate the most victory points in your deck by the time the game ends. A typical game lasts 30 minutes. Dominion won the Spiel des Jahres award in 2009 and is credited with inventing the deck-building genre that has since inspired hundreds of other games.

Each game of Dominion uses only 10 of the 25 available kingdom card types, meaning the combination of cards available creates a different strategic landscape every time. Players must adapt their strategy to the specific cards in play while racing to buy victory cards before the game ends.

## Setup

1. Place the treasure card stacks in the center of the table: Copper (worth 1 coin, costs 0), Silver (worth 2 coins, costs 3), and Gold (worth 3 coins, costs 6). Use 60 Copper cards minus those dealt to players, 40 Silver cards, and 30 Gold cards.
2. Place the victory card stacks: Estate (worth 1 VP, costs 2), Duchy (worth 3 VP, costs 5), and Province (worth 6 VP, costs 8). For 2 players, use 8 of each; for 3-4 players, use 12 of each.
3. Place the Curse cards (worth -1 VP): use 10 for 2 players, 20 for 3, or 30 for 4.
4. Select 10 kingdom card piles for the game. These can be chosen randomly, from a recommended set, or by player agreement. Place 10 copies of each chosen kingdom card in the supply. Kingdom cards provide various actions, extra buys, extra draws, attacks, and other effects.
5. Each player receives a starting deck of 7 Copper cards and 3 Estate cards. Shuffle this 10-card deck and draw 5 cards as your starting hand.
6. Randomly determine the starting player.

## Turn Structure

Each turn follows three phases in order, commonly remembered as ABC (Action, Buy, Cleanup):

1. **Action Phase.** Play one action card from your hand. Action cards have instructions printed on them that may include drawing additional cards (+Cards), gaining additional actions (+Actions), gaining additional buys (+Buys), gaining additional coins (+Coins), attacking other players, or other special effects. If an action card grants +Actions, you may play additional action cards after resolving the first. Without extra actions, you can only play one action card per turn. You may choose to skip this phase.

2. **Buy Phase.** Purchase one card from the supply by spending coins. Your buying power comes from treasure cards in your hand (Copper = 1, Silver = 2, Gold = 3) plus any bonus coins from action cards played this turn. Play all treasure cards you wish to use from your hand. Select a card from the supply with a cost equal to or less than your total coins and place it in your discard pile. If action cards granted +Buys, you may purchase additional cards, spending your remaining coins on each purchase. You may choose not to buy anything.

3. **Cleanup Phase.** Place all cards you played this turn and all cards remaining in your hand into your discard pile. Draw 5 new cards from your deck. If your deck runs out while drawing, shuffle your discard pile to form a new deck and continue drawing. Your turn is now over and the next player begins.

## Win Conditions

The game ends immediately when one of two conditions is met: the Province card pile is empty, or any three supply piles are empty. Players then count the total victory points in their entire deck (including hand, discard pile, and draw deck). Victory points come from Estate cards (1 VP each), Duchy cards (3 VP each), Province cards (6 VP each), Curse cards (-1 VP each), and any kingdom cards that provide victory points. The player with the most victory points wins. In case of a tie, the player who took fewer turns wins. If still tied, the tied players share the victory.

## Components

- 500 cards total in the base game
- 130 basic treasure cards (60 Copper, 40 Silver, 30 Gold)
- 48 basic victory cards (Estates, Duchies, Provinces)
- 30 Curse cards
- 252 kingdom cards (25 different types, 10 copies of each, plus extras)
- 1 trash card (for removed cards)
- 33 placeholder cards for randomizing kingdom selection
- 1 rules booklet
